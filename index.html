About Us

Our innovative approach aims to offer superior respiratory disease pre-screening for patients, clinicians and governments, especially in low-income countries. By providing a rapid, non-invasive, and scalable screening method, this initiative supports the UAE's strategic focus on AI and healthcare innovation, and aims to enhance early detection, resource allocation, and public health preparedness against current and future respiratory threats. Nafasy.ai is a Dubai-based collaborative pilot project uniting Dubai Health and Khalifa University to develop a smartphone app for rapid, cost-effective prescreening of infectious respiratory diseases (e.g., flu, RSV, COVID-19) using AI-powered analysis of cough and breathing sounds. This approach addresses the need for scalable, accessible diagnostic tools, especially for managing outbreaks and improving healthcare resource allocation. The project investigates whether AI analysis of intentional cough sounds can accurately distinguish between healthy individuals and those with respiratory infections, aiming to collect a high-quality dataset from UAE patients and train a proof-of-concept AI algorithm for the UAE market.

Our Mission

We aspire to redefine healthcare by making it anticipatory and preventative through advanced AI. Our goal is to enable fast, reliable diagnostics that empower individuals and providers alike. Our dream is to build a global standard for smart, easy-to-use medical tools.

  • Develop and validate a secure, user-friendly smartphone application for acquiring intentional cough, breathing, and speech sound samples from participants, ensuring compliance with UAE regulations.
  • Prospectively collect a high-quality dataset of intentional audio sounds and associated clinical data from consenting adult patients presenting for gold-standard respiratory panel testing at partner clinics in Dubai (target: minimum 120 participants, at least 50% positive cases).
  • Preprocess collected audio data (noise reduction, segmentation, feature extraction) to ensure quality and suitability for AI model training.
  • Train a proof-of-concept AI algorithm using machine learning techniques to classify individuals as healthy or unhealthy based on features extracted from their cough sounds.
  • Validate the AI model's performance, aiming for at least 75% sensitivity and 75% specificity, against gold-standard respiratory panel tests using appropriate statistical methods.
  • Evaluate the feasibility of this approach for improving respiratory disease surveillance and early detection within the UAE healthcare ecosystem.
  • Support public health preparedness and proactive respiratory disease management across primary care, telemedicine, educational institutions, large events, and transportation hubs.

Partners

Nafasy.ai has ambitious goals for the applications and impact its technology can create globally, and has chosen the UAE as its hub for launch. The project is part of Dubai-based venture development programme Prototypes for Humanity, within which it is in collaboration with Mohammed Bin Rashid University of Medicine and Health Sciences (MBRU), the learning and discovery arm of Dubai Health, as well as Khalifa University.
